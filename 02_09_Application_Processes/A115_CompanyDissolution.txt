Is the application of the company dissolution legal? (§ 65 I 1)
 1. The resolution is made legally. (§ 60 I Nr. 2)
   1.1. The resolution is formally legal.
     case 1.1.1. The resolution is made in a meeting. (§ 48 I)
       case 1.1.1.1. The meeting is personal. (§ 48 I 1)
       case 1.1.1.2. The meeting is a teleconference and all shareholders agree with this format. (§ 48 I 2)
     case 1.1.2. There is no meeting. (§ 48 II)
       case 1.1.2.1. All shareholders consent to the determination in writing (§ 48 II Var. 2)
       case 1.1.2.2. All shareholders agree with a voting in writing (§ 48 II Var. 2)
   1.2. The resolution is made with a qualified majority vote (three quarters). (§ 47 I, § 60 I Nr. 2)
     case 1.2.1. There is no special AoA majority requirement.
       1.2.1.1. Three quarters of the votes are yes. (§ 47 I, § 60 I Nr. 2)
     case 1.2.2. There is a special AoA majority requirement.
       1.2.2.1. Yes ratio is beyond the majority requirement.
     case 1.2.3. All shareholders consent to the determination in writing. (§ 48 II Var. 1)
   1.3. The resolution is notarized if the AoA are amended. (§ 53 II 1, § 60 I Nr. 1, § 3 II)
 2. The liquidators are determined. (§ 66 I)
   case 2.1. The liquidators are determined in the AoA.
   case 2.2. The liquidators are determined in the resolution.
   case 2.3. The liquidators are the directors (default case).
 3. The deed for the appointment of the liquidators is attached. (§ 67 II)
   case 3.1. The deed is original.
   case 3.2. The deed is a certified copy.
 4. The assurances of the liquidators are signed. (§ 67 III)
 5. The applicants are authorized to represent the company. (§ 78, § 35 II 1, § 67 I)
   case 5.1. The resolution entails an AoA amendment.
     5.1.1. The directors are the applicants.
       case 5.1.1.1. One applicant has the sole representation power
         case 5.1.1.1.1. via the general AoA stipulation
         case 5.1.1.1.2. via an individual stipulation
       case 5.1.1.2. One applicant has the modified representation power and another director is present
         case 5.1.1.2.1. via the general AoA stipulation
         case 5.1.1.2.2. via an individual stipulation
       case 5.1.1.3. All directors of the company perform the application.
   case 5.2. The resolution entails no AoA amendment.
     5.2.1. The liquidators are the applicants.
       case 5.2.1.1. One applicant has the sole representation power
         case 5.2.1.1.1. via the general AoA stipulation
         case 5.2.1.1.2. via an individual stipulation
       case 5.2.1.2. One applicant has the modified representation power and another liquidator is present
         case 5.2.1.2.1. via the general AoA stipulation
         case 5.2.1.2.2. via an individual stipulation
       case 5.2.1.3. All liquidators of the company perform the application.
